COMPLIMENT COMPLEMENT

Unsurprisingly, the adjectives relating to compliment and complement also cause confusion. COMPLIMENT When you COMPLIMENT  someone, you are expressing admiration for them, or praising them for something. COMPLEMENT If Mr A complements the skills of Mr B by his skills, the two separate skills (of both the giver and receiver) look better because they are together: they both contribute something that enhances or improves the overall effect. COMPLIMENTARY The speaker received many complimentary remarks from members of the audience. COMPLEMENTARY Complementary is used to describe things that combine in such a way as to enhance or emphasize each other’s good qualities.
